Indira Gandhi, then Prime Minister of India, was assassinated on 31 October 1984 when two of her own Sikh bodyguards, Beant Singh and Satwant Singh, opened fire as she walked through the garden of her residence in New Delhi. Beant Singh fired several shots at her, and after she fell, Satwant Singh continued firing about 30 rounds into her with a submachine gun. Both men then dropped their weapons; Beant was killed by other guards while Satwant was later arrested and executed. The assassination was widely seen as retaliation for her ordering Operation Blue Star earlier that year.
